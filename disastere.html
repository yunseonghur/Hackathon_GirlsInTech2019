Fire


Step1: KEEP your clothes dry. On high heat, wet clothing will scald.

Step2: PUT your shirt over your mouth to keep from inhaling large particulate.

Step3: HIGHTAIL it downhill if possible, and stay low to the ground. Fire moves uphill faster because of updrafts, and smoke rises.

Step4: CONSIDER wind direction: if its blowing toward the fire from where you are, run into the wind. (Hint: if it is blowing from behind toward you, run perpendicular to the wind.)
Step5: AVOID canyons and chutes, which can funnel deadly heat towards you as you descend.

Step6: MOVE to an area that has already burned and is less likely to speak again if you are caught out in the open.
